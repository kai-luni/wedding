Internet & Communication

SIM Cards

At Suvarnabhumi Airport in the baggage claim hall, there's an excellent SIM card shop near Carousel 19. Here you can buy unlimited internet packages depending on how long you're staying. The friendly staff will either insert the physical SIM card into your phone or help you set up an eSIM directly - very convenient!

Packages are available from major providers (AIS, DTAC, TrueMove) starting from 300-500 THB. You can also find SIM cards at convenience stores throughout the city if you prefer to get one later.

Money & Payment

How to Pay

Cash is king for tourists in Thailand! While locals commonly use QR code scan apps for payments (like PromptPay), these digital payment systems aren't easily accessible for tourists. Most local restaurants, street vendors, and smaller shops expect cash payments. Credit cards are accepted at larger establishments, but they often come with high fees, so it's best to rely primarily on cash during your stay.

Getting Cash in Thailand

Bring Thai Baht from Germany: Your bank in Germany can exchange Euros for Thai Baht before you travel. This is often convenient and gives you cash ready to use upon arrival.

ATMs in Thailand: You can withdraw cash from ATMs throughout Thailand, but be aware that fees are quite high and vary between different banks and ATM operators.

Restaurants & Food

SoupSoup Restaurant

We highly recommend trying this unique restaurant experience! Here's how it works: when you enter, you choose from a variety of fresh ingredients that go into a bowl. At the cash register, you then select the cooking style for your ingredients. From the pictures below, you can see two popular options: Japanese shabu soup and fried kimchi. Once you've made your selections, the finished meal will be brought directly to your table.

It's a fun, interactive dining experience that lets you customize your meal exactly how you want it!
