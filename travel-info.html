Arrival in Thailand

Online Arrival Card

Important: You must fill out the online arrival card 3 days before your arrival in Thailand. This is a mandatory requirement for all international visitors.

Complete the form at: tdac.immigration.go.th/arrival-card

Information you'll need:

  • Your flight number for the flight arriving in Bangkok
  • Address of your first hotel in Bangkok
  • Your passport number

Make sure to complete this form in advance to avoid any delays during immigration processing!

Transportation in Thailand

Local Transportation

Tuk Tuks: Fun to experience but overpriced! Great for short rides and photos, but don't rely on them for regular transportation.

Taxis: Fine and cheap for getting around the city. Avoid the waiting taxis in front of malls or tourist areas, and always make sure they activate the taximeter before starting your journey.

Motorcycle Taxis: An adventure for the brave! These are fast and can navigate through traffic, but make sure to negotiate the price beforehand. Hold on tight!

Grab Taxi: Relatively expensive nowadays, but it's a secure and convenient way to get from A to B. Good for when you want reliability and don't want to negotiate.

Long Distance Travel

Minivan: This is what the Thais use a lot for far distance travel. Very cheap and reliable - a great way to travel like a local!

Overnight Train: My personal favorite! They are slow but convenient, and you'll arrive at your destination in the morning. It's a unique experience and you save on a night's accommodation.

Internet & Communication

SIM Cards

At Suvarnabhumi Airport in the baggage claim hall, there's an excellent SIM card shop near Carousel 19. Here you can buy unlimited internet packages depending on how long you're staying. The friendly staff will either insert the physical SIM card into your phone or help you set up an eSIM directly - very convenient!

Packages are available from major providers (AIS, DTAC, TrueMove) starting from 300-500 THB. You can also find SIM cards at convenience stores throughout the city if you prefer to get one later.

Money & Payment

How to Pay

Cash is king for tourists in Thailand! While locals commonly use QR code scan apps for payments (like PromptPay), these digital payment systems aren't easily accessible for tourists. Most local restaurants, street vendors, and smaller shops expect cash payments.

Credit Cards: You can use credit cards in many shops and larger establishments, but fees vary significantly by store. For example, 7-Eleven charges a 200 baht fee every time you use a credit card, while stores like Decathlon don't charge any additional fees. Because of these variable and sometimes high fees, it's best to use credit cards only when necessary and rely primarily on cash for most purchases.

Getting Cash in Thailand

Bring Thai Baht from Germany: Your bank in Germany can exchange Euros for Thai Baht before you travel. This is often convenient and gives you cash ready to use upon arrival.

ATMs in Thailand: You can withdraw cash from ATMs throughout Thailand, but be aware that fees are quite high and vary between different banks and ATM operators.

Restaurants & Food

SoupSoup Restaurant

We highly recommend trying this unique restaurant experience! Here's how it works: when you enter, you choose from a variety of fresh ingredients that go into a bowl. At the cash register, you then select the cooking style for your ingredients. From the pictures below, you can see two popular options: Japanese shabu soup and fried kimchi. Once you've made your selections, the finished meal will be brought directly to your table.

It's a fun, interactive dining experience that lets you customize your meal exactly how you want it!

Restaurant Location: maps.app.goo.gl/BhrAdpwMNQdBi7Sa7

Kin Lom Chom Saphan

A beautiful rooftop restaurant in the old city with stunning river views. Perfect for sunset or evening dining with excellent seafood and live music to enhance the atmosphere. The combination of great food, scenic views, and entertainment makes this a memorable dining experience.

Restaurant Location: maps.app.goo.gl/TywbCGA6mCz4YhTL8

Steve Cafe & Cuisine

Specializing in authentic Thai southern food, this riverside restaurant offers a unique culinary experience. Located next to the river, it's an ideal spot to enjoy the regional flavors of southern Thailand while taking in the peaceful waterfront setting.

Restaurant Location: maps.app.goo.gl/oMEXrg5e2wSfz9b27

Methavalai Sorndaeng

Centrally located in the old city, this restaurant is perfect for escaping the heat of the day. They serve excellent Thai food with an English menu, making it convenient for international visitors while maintaining authentic flavors.

Restaurant Location: maps.app.goo.gl/ArQ8qq9oArrJr5nF7

Pier 21

Located in Terminal 21 shopping mall, this food court offers a great variety of affordable food options. It's perfect for trying different dishes without breaking the budget, and you can enjoy some shopping before or after your meal in this modern mall setting.

Restaurant Location: maps.app.goo.gl/RXU3p8z6TYUT7TG5A

White Flower Cafe

A charming cafe with a lovely atmosphere, perfect for a relaxing brunch experience. Known for their excellent cakes and delicious Thai food, White Flower Cafe offers a great combination of sweet treats and authentic local cuisine in a cozy setting.

Restaurant Location: maps.app.goo.gl/qC16xPUAPj4zDE9J7
