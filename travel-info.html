Essential Travel Information

We've put together all the important details to help make your journey to Thailand as smooth as possible. From visa requirements to local customs, here's what you need to know!

Visa Requirements

Tourist Visa on Arrival

Most European countries can enter Thailand visa-free for up to 30 days (when arriving by air) or 15 days (when arriving by land). Check the current requirements for your specific nationality before traveling.

Required Documents

  • Passport valid for at least 6 months from entry date
  • Return flight ticket or onward travel proof
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel booking confirmation)
  • Sufficient funds (approximately 20,000 THB or equivalent)

Important: Visa requirements can change, so please check with the Thai embassy in your country or the official Thai immigration website before traveling.

Flight Information

Main Airport: Suvarnabhumi Airport (BKK)

This is Bangkok's main international airport and the best option for most international flights. It's about 45-60 minutes to central Bangkok depending on traffic.

Alternative: Don Mueang Airport (DMK)

Mainly serves budget airlines and domestic flights. About 45 minutes to central Bangkok.

Flight Booking Tips

  • Book flights 2-3 months in advance for better prices
  • Consider flying midweek (Tuesday-Thursday) for lower fares
  • Popular airlines: Thai Airways, Lufthansa, Emirates, Qatar Airways
  • Flight time from Europe: approximately 11-13 hours

Airport to City Transportation

Airport Rail Link (Recommended)

Fast and affordable train service from Suvarnabhumi Airport to central Bangkok. Takes about 30 minutes to Phaya Thai station. Cost: approximately 45 THB.

Taxi

Convenient door-to-door service. Use the official taxi queue on Level 1. Cost: 400-600 THB plus tolls (approximately 50-100 THB). Journey time: 45-90 minutes depending on traffic.

Grab (App-based)

Download the Grab app for convenient ride-hailing. Similar cost to taxis but with upfront pricing.

Airport Bus

Budget option with various routes to different parts of Bangkok. Cost: 35-60 THB. Takes longer due to multiple stops.

  • Bank exchange counters: Best rates, available at airports and throughout the city

Payment Methods

Cash is still king in Thailand, especially for street food, taxis, and small vendors. Credit cards are accepted at hotels, restaurants, and shopping malls.

Local Transportation in Bangkok

BTS Skytrain & MRT Subway

Modern, clean, and efficient rail systems covering most of Bangkok. Get a Rabbit Card for easy travel. Cost: 16-59 THB per journey.

Taxis & Grab

Affordable and convenient. Always ask for the meter to be turned on in taxis, or use Grab for transparent pricing.

Tuk-tuks

Fun for short distances and tourist areas. Always negotiate the price before starting your journey.

River Boats

Great for traveling along the Chao Phraya River. Fast and scenic way to reach many attractions.

Health & Safety

Vaccinations

No specific vaccinations are required for travelers from Europe. Consider routine vaccines (MMR, Hepatitis A/B) and consult your doctor for personal recommendations.

Health Tips

  • Drink bottled or filtered water
  • Use mosquito repellent, especially in the evenings
  • Be cautious with street food if you have a sensitive stomach
  • Carry hand sanitizer

Travel Insurance

Highly recommended! Ensure your policy covers medical expenses, trip cancellation, and lost luggage.

Emergency Numbers

  • Tourist Police: 1155
  • General Emergency: 191
  • Medical Emergency: 1669

Cultural Tips & Etiquette

Respect for the Royal Family

Thai people deeply respect their monarchy. Avoid any negative comments about the royal family.

Buddhist Temples

Dress modestly when visiting temples (cover shoulders and knees). Remove shoes before entering temple buildings.

Greetings

The traditional "wai" greeting involves placing palms together and bowing slightly. A smile and nod are also perfectly acceptable for foreigners.

Tipping

Not mandatory but appreciated. 10-15% at restaurants, round up taxi fares, 20-50 THB for hotel staff.

Useful Thai Phrases

  • Hello: Sawasdee (ka/krub)
  • Thank you: Khob khun (ka/krub)
  • Excuse me: Khor thot
  • How much?: Tao rai?
  • Where is...?: Yoo tee nai?

Note: Women say "ka" and men say "krub" at the end of phrases for politeness.

Internet & Communication

SIM Cards

At Suvarnabhumi Airport in the baggage claim hall, there's an excellent SIM card shop near Carousel 19. Here you can buy unlimited internet packages depending on how long you're staying. The friendly staff will either insert the physical SIM card into your phone or help you set up an eSIM directly - very convenient!

Packages are available from major providers (AIS, DTAC, TrueMove) starting from 300-500 THB. You can also find SIM cards at convenience stores throughout the city if you prefer to get one later.

WiFi

Free WiFi is widely available in hotels, restaurants, cafes, and shopping malls. Most accommodations provide reliable internet access.

Useful Apps

  • Grab: Transportation and food delivery
  • Google Translate: Camera function works great for Thai text
  • Google Maps: Excellent for navigation in Bangkok
  • BTS/MRT apps: For public transportation routes

Shopping & Souvenirs

What to Buy

  • Thai silk products
  • Handicrafts and artwork
  • Thai spices and tea
  • Thai skincare products
  • Traditional Thai jewelry

Where to Shop

  • Chatuchak Weekend Market: Huge market with everything imaginable
  • MBK Center: Popular shopping mall
  • Siam Paragon: Upscale shopping center
  • Floating Markets: Unique shopping experience

Bargaining

Expected at markets and street vendors. Start at about 50% of the asking price and negotiate friendly. Fixed prices in malls and department stores.

Food & Dining

Thai Cuisine Highlights

  • Pad Thai: Classic stir-fried noodles
  • Tom Yum Goong: Spicy shrimp soup
  • Green Curry: Creamy and aromatic curry
  • Mango Sticky Rice: Perfect dessert
  • Som Tam: Spicy papaya salad

Where to Eat

  • Street Food: Authentic and affordable, look for busy stalls
  • Food Courts: Great variety in shopping malls
  • Local Restaurants: Ask hotel staff for recommendations
  • Night Markets: Perfect for dinner and atmosphere

Spice Levels

Thai food can be very spicy! Ask for "mai pet" (not spicy) or "pet nit noi" (a little spicy) if you're sensitive to heat.

Time Zone & Contact

Time Zone

Thailand is UTC+7 (no daylight saving time). During European summer time, Thailand is 5-6 hours ahead of Europe.
